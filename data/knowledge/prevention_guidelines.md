# Customer Safety & Prevention Guidelines

A comprehensive salon safety knowledge base outlines protocols for sanitization, chemical handling, and client well-being. Sharing these guidelines builds customer trust and ensures a clean, hazard-free environment.

## 1. Hygiene & Sanitation Protocols
* **Tool Disinfection**: All metal tools (e.g., scissors, combs, clippers, tweezers) must be cleaned with soap and then submerged in an EPA-registered hospital-grade disinfectant between every client.
* **Single-Use Items**: Single-use items like neck strips, sponges, and waxing spatulas must be discarded immediately after a single use.
* **Clean Linens**: Capes and towels must be laundered in hot water and detergent after each client.
* **Hand Hygiene**: Stylists and technicians must wash their hands or use hand sanitizer before and after every appointment.
* **Workstation Wipe-downs**: All high-touch surfaces, including hydraulic chairs, mirrors, and countertops, must be disinfected between appointments.

## 2. Chemical Safety & Allergy Prevention
* **Product Safety**: Staff should follow strict chemical handling rules. Always read product labels, store flammable materials properly, and use adequate ventilation to prevent inhalation of fumes.
* **Patch Tests**: Perform patch tests 24-48 hours before applying hair color or chemical treatments to ensure clients do not suffer allergic reactions.
* **Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)**: Staff must wear appropriate PPE—such as disposable nitrile/vinyl gloves and aprons—when mixing or applying chemical solutions.
* **First Aid**: Keep a well-stocked first-aid kit on-site and train all staff on how to quickly handle product exposure or skin irritations.

## 3. Client Guidelines & Etiquette
* **Appointments Only**: Encourage clients to pre-book appointments to prevent overcrowding and maintain proper social distancing in the waiting area.
* **Health Screening**: Request that clients reschedule their appointments if they are feeling unwell, running a fever, or experiencing contagious symptoms.
* **Belongings & Payments**: Encourage clients to minimize the personal items they bring into the salon and to opt for digital, contactless payments.
* **Client Handwashing**: Provide a clear hand-sanitizer station at the entrance and politely instruct clients to sanitize or wash their hands upon arrival.

## 4. Hazard Prevention
* **Slips and Trips**: Keep walkways completely clear of debris, electrical cords, and product packaging to prevent falls. Promptly clean up any wet floors or product spills.
* **Electrical Equipment**: Ensure hairdryers, straighteners, and clippers are well-maintained, stored safely when not in use, and regularly inspected for damaged cords.
